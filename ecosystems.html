What Are Ecosystems?

An ecosystem is a community of living organisms (plants, animals, microbes) interacting with their physical environment (soil, water, air). These systems maintain balance through nutrient cycles and energy flow, supporting biodiversity and life on Earth. Click the images below to explore!

Types of Ecosystems

Ecosystems vary widely, from forests and grasslands to wetlands and coral reefs. Each plays a unique role in maintaining global health. For example, wetlands filter water, while coral reefs protect coastlines. Explore these ecosystems below!

Why Ecosystems Matter

Ecosystems provide essential services like clean air, food, and climate regulation. Human activities, such as deforestation and pollution, threaten them. Protecting ecosystems ensures a sustainable future. Click below to learn how ecosystems support life!
